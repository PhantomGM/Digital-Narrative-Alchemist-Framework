# Alignment System System

Pathfinder 2nd Edition utilizes the classic 9-grid alignment system, structured around two axes:
1. **Moral Axis**: Good vs. Evil
2. **Ethical Axis**: Lawful vs. Chaotic

## The Nine Alignments
- **Lawful Good (LG)**: Compassion combined with honor and duty. e.g. Paladins of Iomedae.
- **Neutral Good (NG)**: Doing what is right, regardless of rules.
- **Chaotic Good (CG)**: Freedom and benevolence. Breaking rules to help people.
- **Lawful Neutral (LN)**: Order, tradition, and rules are paramount.
- **True Neutral (N)**: Balance, pragmatism, or simply lacking strong convictions.
- **Chaotic Neutral (CN)**: Ultimate freedom, unpredictability, following personal whims.
- **Lawful Evil (LE)**: Tyranny, exploitation within the confines of law or hierarchy. e.g. Asmodeus.
- **Neutral Evil (NE)**: Pure selfishness, willing to do whatever it takes to get ahead.
- **Chaotic Evil (CE)**: Destructive, cruel, and arbitrary. e.g. Demons.

## Mechanical Implications
Alignment is a tangible force in Golarion.
- **Damage Types**: Certain spells and weapons deal `good`, `evil`, `lawful`, or `chaotic` damage. These damage types ONLY harm creatures of the opposing alignment. (e.g., A `good` aligned Holy sword deals extra damage to Evil creatures, but does zero extra damage to a Neutral creature).
- **Deity Edicts and Anathema**: Clerics and Champions must adhere to their deity's alignment restrictions, edicts (things they must do), and anathema (things they must never do).
