# PF2E Roleplay Rules and Mechanics

Roleplay in PF2E is deeply supported by explicit mechanics, primarily through Skill Actions and NPC Attitudes.

## NPC Attitudes
NPCs track their disposition toward PCs on a 5-step scale:
1. **Helpful**: Will actively aid the PCs without requiring much.
2. **Friendly**: Likely to agree to safe, simple requests.
3. **Indifferent**: Does not care either way. (Default state for most strangers).
4. **Unfriendly**: Dislikes the PCs, will not accept requests.
5. **Hostile**: Actively seeks to harm or thwart the PCs.

## Key Social Actions
Players use the 3-action economy even in social encounters (often extrapolated into exploration mode):

- **Make an Impression (Diplomacy)**: Takes 1 minute of conversation to try to improve an NPC's attitude. Critical success improves attitude by 2 steps; success by 1 step. Critical failure worsens it by 1 step.
- **Request (Diplomacy)**: Ask a Friendly or Helpful NPC to do something.
- **Coerce (Intimidation)**: Threaten an NPC into complying. Success forces compliance for a short time, but the NPC later becomes Unfriendly or Hostile.
- **Lie (Deception)**: Trick an NPC. Checked against their Perception DC.
- **Create a Diversion (Deception)**: Draw attention away to allow sneaking or repositioning.

## Roleplay Ethos
The GM (or DNA Arbiter) should reward players for engaging with NPC attitudes rather than just rolling dice. The world reacts dynamically: saving an Innkeeper adjusts their attitude to Helpful, granting discounts, while Coercing them provides short-term gain but long-term narrative penalties.
